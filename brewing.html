Welcome back! We already touched on sanitation and maintaining proper temperature. Today I'd like to talk about transferring your brew from primary to secondary fermentation. First I'd like to point out the reason why this is necessary, and that is to remove waste from the primary fermentation and to promote further fermentation. The first reason is very important as this helps to clarify your brew and mitigate cloudiness, the second part is also important as you don't want want whatever remaining yeast to get contaminated by the waste of the primary fermentation. As you can see there is a video to the right that will walk any new brewers through the process of transferring their batch from primary to secondary fermentation.
